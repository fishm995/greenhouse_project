About the Greenhouse System

Discover the innovative Smart Greenhouse Management System developed by MACC's ITC281 class.

Our Mission

The Greenhouse Management System is a collaborative project by the ITC281 class at Moberly Area Community College (MACC), guided by Dr. Semi Necibi. Our goal is to bridge technology and agriculture through a web-based platform that enables remote monitoring and control of greenhouse environments. From irrigation and temperature regulation to air quality and soil moisture tracking, our system optimizes plant growth while showcasing the technical skills we've honed at MACC.

Key Features

Plant Growth Tracking

Monitor soil moisture, temperature, and light intensity to ensure optimal plant health.

Remote Access

Control greenhouse systems from anywhere using our intuitive web interface.

Historical Data Analysis

Access logged data to analyze trends and improve greenhouse performance.

Login & User Management

Secure access with role-based user management for administrators and users.

Our Vision

The Greenhouse Management System, developed for the greenhouses at Moberly Area Community College (MACC), is a web-based platform designed to facilitate monitoring and management of a single greenhouse, with potential for future expansion. It enables both students and teachers to interact with greenhouse operations, allowing real-time monitoring of environmental conditions such as temperature, irrigation, air quality, and soil moisture, as well as control of devices. To maintain the stability of the greenhouse environment, advanced management functions are restricted to teachers, while students can engage with core monitoring and basic control features. This system fosters collaboration and hands-on learning while ensuring a stable and optimized greenhouse environment.
